## Why Use the Customer Lifetime Value (LTV) Calculator?

Understanding Customer Lifetime Value (LTV) allows SaaS executives to determine how much gross profit each customer generates over their lifetime. This metric sets maximum budget caps for Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC) and dictates capital allocation.

Having accurate, real-time insights into this metric helps SaaS founders, CFOs, and growth teams optimize capital allocation, protect operating runway, and achieve top-tier venture scalability.

## Mathematical Formula & Derivation

The mathematical derivation for this financial metric is expressed as:

$$\text{Average Customer Lifespan (Months)} = \frac{1}{\text{Monthly Churn Rate}}$$ \n$$\text{LTV} = \frac{\text{ARPU} \times \text{Gross Margin \%}}{\text{Monthly Churn Rate} - \text{Monthly Expansion Rate}}$$

Where all input values are evaluated over a standardized monthly or annual accounting period.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### What is Customer Lifetime Value (LTV)?
LTV measures the total net gross profit generated by a single customer across their entire active relationship with your SaaS company.

### Why include gross margin in the LTV formula?
Gross revenue overstates customer value because serving subscribers incurs hosting and support costs. Margin-adjusted LTV reflects true net profitability.

### How does expansion revenue boost LTV?
Expansion revenue (upsells, seat expansion) reduces net churn. When expansion exceeds customer churn, net churn becomes negative, driving exponential compounding LTV.

### What is a healthy customer churn benchmark for SaaS?
For SMB SaaS, a monthly churn of 2-3% is standard. For Enterprise SaaS, monthly churn under 0.5% to 1.0% is target.

### How can SaaS businesses increase LTV?
Companies can boost LTV by raising ARPU through usage-based pricing, improving customer onboarding to reduce churn, and introducing cross-sell feature modules.

### How does LTV relate to CAC?
LTV measures total return while CAC measures acquisition cost. Healthy unit economics demand an LTV of at least 3x CAC.

No. All calculations run strictly in your browser.
